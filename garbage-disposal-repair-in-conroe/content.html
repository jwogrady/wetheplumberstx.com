Garbage Disposal Care Tips

Grease buildup, food waste, and everyday wear can all lead to clogs and jams. To keep your disposal running smoothly:

  • Run cold water before, during, and after each use to help food particles flush cleanly through your pipes.

  • Avoid pouring fats or oils down the drain, as they solidify inside pipes and lead to blockages.

  • Feed waste slowly rather than dumping large amounts at once, which helps prevent overloading the motor.

  • Clean regularly by grinding ice cubes and citrus peels to remove buildup and neutralize odors.

How to Prevent Garbage Disposal Clogs

Preventing clogs is easier than dealing with them later. A few mindful habits can extend the life of your disposal and your entire kitchen drain line:

  • Keep these items out: fibrous vegetables (celery, corn husks, asparagus), starchy foods (pasta, rice, potatoes), coffee grounds, eggshells, grease, oils, fruit pits, and bones.

  • Use cold water only. It keeps grease solid so it can pass through the pipes instead of coating them.

  • Clean monthly with ice cubes and vinegar or citrus peels to reduce buildup and eliminate odors.

  • Never use chemical drain cleaners. They can corrode the disposal and pipes. Choose enzyme-based cleaners or schedule a professional cleaning instead.
