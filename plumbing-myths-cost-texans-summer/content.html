Top Plumbing Myths That Drain Texans’ Wallets

Some plumbing myths sound reasonable. But in reality, they cost Texas homeowners time, money, and peace of mind.

One of the most common myths is that all wipes are flushable. Despite the label, most wipes don’t break down like toilet paper. With heavier summer use and more people in the home, these wipes clog pipes fast. Clearing them can mean digging up your yard or replacing whole sections of pipe.

Another common belief is that leaky faucets can wait. With the extra water use in summer, even a slow drip adds up fast. In areas with hard water, like much of Texas, minerals build up inside plumbing fixtures and worsen the problem. Delaying repairs often means bigger issues later.

A favorite kitchen myth is that ice cubes sharpen garbage disposal blades. Garbage disposals don’t use sharp blades; they use blunt impellers. Ice can help clean the unit but won’t sharpen anything. Believing this myth may distract from more important maintenance tasks.

Many people assume outdoor plumbing is safe in summer. But the sun and heat can crack hoses, spigots, and fittings. Ignoring these systems just because it’s hot outside can lead to costly leaks and water waste.

Lastly, chemical drain cleaners may seem like an easy fix, but they are harsh on pipes. They corrode older plumbing and can harm septic systems. Over time, repeated use leads to more clogs and more costly repairs.

Double-checking advice with a licensed plumber helps you avoid expensive mistakes. If something seems off, don’t wait to get it checked out.

Summer Plumbing Facts Every Texan Should Know

Hot weather stresses plumbing. But knowing what to look for helps you stay ahead of problems.

Water use climbs in the summer. Extra demand exposes leaks, cracks, and weak spots. Check your home regularly for rust, stains, dripping faucets, and slow drains.

Texas hard water causes minerals to build up quickly, especially during high-use months. Watch for low water pressure and visible scale around faucets. Addressing this early keeps appliances and pipes running well.

Don’t ignore slow drains. Summer brings more showers, more laundry, and more food waste. These can all add up to serious blockages. Skip the drain chemicals and call in help if the problem keeps returning.

Outdoor faucets and hoses need inspection too. Heat and pressure can cause small cracks that leak for weeks without being noticed. A small leak can waste hundreds of gallons in just a few days.

Always remember: wipes, paper towels, and hygiene products should go in the trash, not the toilet. Flush only human waste and toilet paper.

Schedule a plumbing check-up before and after the summer rush. Preventative care costs less than emergency calls.

Top 5 Plumbing Myths Texans Should Ignore

  1. All wipes are flushable. They aren’t. Even labeled wipes can block your sewer line.
  2. Grease is safe with hot water. Grease cools and hardens in your pipes.
  3. If it’s not leaking loudly, it’s not a problem. Quiet leaks still cause damage.
  4. A slow drain is harmless. It usually means a bigger clog is forming.
  5. Chemical cleaners are safe. They can eat through your pipes over time.

Skip these myths. Talk to a licensed plumber instead.

Simple Habits for Summer Plumbing Success

Check faucets and pipes monthly for leaks. After storms, inspect outdoor spigots and hose connections.

Don’t flush wipes or pour grease down drains. Use trash bins and grease containers.

Flush your water heater mid-summer to remove sediment. Check for rust, water stains, or moisture.

Watch for condensation or damp spots in attics, garages, and crawl spaces. These are early signs of pipe trouble.

Fact-check all plumbing tips. Use sources like licensed plumbers or state plumbing boards. Social media isn’t always right.
